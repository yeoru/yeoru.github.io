"Flow and Shield"(2024) · Artience Daejeon × KRIBB


"Flow and Shield" is an interactive installation that explores themes of viruses and immunity, where desire is understood as a force that spreads, transforms, and mutates across bodies, media environments, and collective imagination.

Visitors physically interact with a rotating wooden structure, pushing and pulling it with their bodies to generate virus-like digital forms in real time.

The wooden structure also functions as a system of defense and release: participants can either protect their desire-objects by controlling the structure, or let them re-enter the field of contagion.

The projected entities on the floor are capable of simultaneously recognizing multiple participants. Among them, the most dominant entity gradually absorbs and transforms the others through a process of viral assimilation, where weaker entities are drawn into its influence and partially integrated.

In some cases, this dynamic leads to the emergence of a single dominant entity remaining on the surface, shaped by continuous competition and adaptation within the system.

When a participant is detected by the camera, corresponding sounds are triggered, creating a responsive and immersive environment where movement, image, sound, and bodily interaction continuously affect one another.


PC, projector, Kinect sensor, rotating wooden installation, live screening, sound, variable dimensions, 2024

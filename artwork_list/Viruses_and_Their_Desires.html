Viruses and their Desires(2024) · Artience Daejeon × KRIBB


9 speculative short-form narratives operating as a multi-voiced speculative narrative system in a dialogic, speaker-labeled format. Structured as an internet-article–based ecosystem, these texts form a conceptual framework for real-time digital interaction. Developed with KRIBB and AI, they function as a bridge between speculative fiction and the Infected Desires installation. Each entry is constructed as a multi-agent narrative in which non-human voices co-produce discourse through a structured utterance system. Rather than traditional storytelling, the work operates as a narrative interface where scientific observation merges with fictional imagination, producing a hybrid language of virology and speculative discourse. Within this framework, desire becomes a mode of survival and perception: for example, just as humans construct “safe assets” through concepts like secure real estate investment, viruses—prior to the emergence of COVID-19 antimicrobial films—perceived surfaces such as elevator buttons as stable nodes of transmission, akin to ecological safe zones. At the end of each narrative, the trajectory of desire is documented through diagrammatic graphs and hand-drawn scribbles.
